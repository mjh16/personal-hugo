Awp 2015

So I’m currently in Minneapolis for the 2015 awp conference and am having a marvelous time. The picture above is of the bookfair on Thursday morning before it got super crazy. That place has been an overwhelming and amazing experience. I’ve only walked through maybe a third of it so far between sessions. I plan to spend more time there tomorrow.

Today I got to see some very interesting panels on video essays and things that I actually really enjoy writing and doing, so that was good. It was especially enlightening to hear Eula Biss and John Breslan talk process for drafting and editing video essays. While I still don’t agree with all of their choices in their work, having the opportunity to look behind the curtain gave me a deeper understanding and context that I didn’t get from viewing the work online.

I’ve also been able to attend some other really great panels, so expect a fuller report at some point. While stressful in the context of this spring, the conference has been really enlightening and productive. I’ve especially enjoyed the work that I’ve seen and heard that tease, in productive ways, at the same borders and boundaries that I’m interested in. I’m glad to see that there are others mining the same grey area.  

I’ve also been able to drink some good local beer, so that’s been awesome.
